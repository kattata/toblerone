ABOUT THE NAME

The name 'Toblerone' is a combination of the chocolatier's family name 'Tobler' and 'torrone', which means 'nougat' in Italian.

ABOUT THE SHAPE

Some say that the triangular shape of Toblerone was inspired by the mountains surrounding the Tobler family's home, but in fact it was the dancers of Folies Bergères in Paris who finish their show in a pyramid that initiated this worldwide known silhouette.

1868

Jean Tobler opens his confectionery shop in Bern, Switzerland. He's still reliable on other manufacturers.

1899

Their chocolate is doing so great that Jean decides to found his own factory with his sons, which he calls „Fabrique de Chocolat Berne, Tobler & Cie”.

1900

Jean Tobler hands the company over to his son Theodor Tobler

1908

Theodor and his cousin Emil Baumann invent Toblerone with its unique shape and recognizable logo.

1909

Theodor applies for a patent for his product. It becomes the first patented milk chocolate bar with almonds and honey.

1969

Dark Toblerone chocolate launches

1973

White Toblerone chocolate launches

2007

For the first time in 34 years, a completely new product launches - Fruit & Nut Toblerone.
